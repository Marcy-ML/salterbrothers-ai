Key insights from the session

What the room told us.

The depth of engagement and the questions asked revealed four strategic priorities that should shape the next phase of work.

◆
Cross-organisational data is the urgent gap
Portfolio managers operate between enterprise brands and guests with limited access to the customer intelligence that lives inside IHG and Accor systems. This is structural, not technical — and solvable through negotiation as much as technology.
◆
Worlds Apart is the clean starting point
As a fully owned and operated portfolio, Salter Brothers controls the experience end to end. The AI discoverability gap, the data foundation, and the GEO strategy are all within direct control. This is where the flywheel starts.
◆
Technology lock-in is an immediate risk
Pressure test SaaS and agentic solutions claiming AI-native capability to ensure they do not create vendor dependency or integration challenges. A broader data intelligence strategy will provide a true north and durable foundations for a roadmap — before anything is signed.
◆
Digital twin and ontology resonated at board level
The concept of a digital twin for portfolio simulation, and the idea of structured data ontology enabling a board pack, were the two ideas that generated the most excitement. Both are entry points for a more strategic engagement.
