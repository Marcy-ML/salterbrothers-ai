# AI Buzzword Decoder
## Salter Brothers · AI Leadership Offsite · May 2026
### A plain-English reference for every AI term you'll encounter

---

## The Fundamentals

**AI (Artificial Intelligence)**
Computers doing tasks that used to require human thinking — understanding language, recognising patterns, making decisions. An umbrella term covering many different capabilities.
*For your properties: demand forecasting, energy optimisation, guest personalisation.*

**Analytical AI**
AI that finds patterns in your own data to predict outcomes. Occupancy forecasting, revenue management, energy scheduling. You already use elements of this.
*This is AI working on your data. It doesn't know anything about the outside world.*

**Generative AI (GenAI)**
AI that creates new content — text, images, responses — based on what it learned from vast amounts of human knowledge. ChatGPT, Claude and Gemini are all generative AI.
*Old search showed you results. Generative AI has a conversation with you.*

**LLM (Large Language Model)**
The engine powering ChatGPT, Claude and Gemini. Trained on enormous amounts of text to understand and generate human language. Each company builds its own — GPT-4, Claude Sonnet, Gemini — same concept, different models.
*The technology your future guests are already using to plan their travel.*

**Agentic AI**
AI that takes action — not just answers questions. An agentic AI doesn't wait to be asked; it monitors, decides and acts across systems on your behalf. A concierge who recommends AND books the table AND arranges the car.
*The invisible chief of staff across your portfolio — operating 24/7.*

**AI Agent**
A specific AI system built to accomplish a defined set of tasks autonomously. You can have multiple agents — a Guest Intelligence Agent, a Revenue Agent, an Event Agent — all working from the same data layer.
*Think of each agent as a specialist who never sleeps.*

## How AI Finds Your Hotel

**GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation)**
The new SEO — making sure AI engines can find your property, understand it accurately, and recommend it confidently. SEO was for Google. GEO is for ChatGPT, Claude and Perplexity.
*Without GEO: AI either doesn't recommend you or describes you incorrectly. With GEO: you appear accurately and compellingly in AI-mediated travel planning.*

**AI Search / AI Overviews**
When AI engines answer travel questions directly — "recommend a luxury wellness retreat in regional Australia" — without the guest ever clicking through to a website. If you're not in the answer, you don't exist in their consideration set.
*69% of searches are now zero-click. The answer arrives before anyone clicks.*

**Zero-Click**
When a guest gets their answer from AI without visiting any website. The guest journey now often starts and ends inside an AI conversation. Hotels not optimised for this are invisible in the new discovery layer.
*Your future guest may never visit your website. They may book directly from an AI conversation.*

**MCP (Model Context Protocol)**
The open technical standard that lets AI engines connect directly to your live hotel data — rates, availability, amenities — instead of guessing from outdated web scrapes. Think of it as the USB-C connector for AI.
*Without MCP: AI shows guests old rates and may send them to OTAs. With MCP: AI checks your live inventory in real time.*

**GDS (Global Distribution System)**
The infrastructure travel agents used to access hotel inventory. MCP is the equivalent for the AI era — the same concept, built for AI systems instead of human travel agents.

## The Data Architecture

**Data Intelligence Layer**
A unified, governed layer that connects all your property data — PMS, POS, IoT, guest profiles, events — into a single trusted source that AI agents, workflows and experiences can draw from. Your most important strategic asset.
*This is your moat. Models commoditise. Your proprietary guest intelligence does not.*

**API (Application Programming Interface)**
How software systems talk to each other. Your booking engine has an API. Your channel manager has an API. For AI to access your live data, every platform you choose must have a well-documented, open API.
*The non-negotiable test for every SaaS or platform decision: is it API-first?*

**Headless Architecture**
A technology design where the front-end (what guests see) is separated from the back-end (where data lives), connected only by APIs. This gives you freedom to plug in new AI capabilities without rebuilding your entire stack.
*Headless = future-proof. Monolithic = locked in.*

**API-First**
A platform built from the ground up to share data openly with other systems. The opposite of vendor lock-in. Every technology decision you make today should pass this test.
*If a vendor can't clearly explain their API, walk away.*

**IoT (Internet of Things)**
The network of sensors and connected devices throughout your property — HVAC, environmental controls, energy systems, occupancy sensors. The raw data that powers operational intelligence and predictive maintenance.
*Your property is already generating intelligence. Most of it is going unread.*

**Edge Computing**
Processing data at or near the source (in the property) rather than sending everything to a central cloud. Faster, lower cost, and essential for real-time operational decisions.
*The intelligence layer that makes your digital twin possible.*

**Digital Twin**
A real-time virtual model of your property that mirrors actual operations. Allows you to simulate decisions — "what happens to spa yield if we move check-in to 1pm?" — before committing.
*Simulate before you act. The general manager's most powerful tool.*

**Ontology**
A structured map of how all your data concepts relate to each other — guests, rooms, events, services, preferences — so AI agents can reason across them coherently. Palantir Foundry's core concept.
*The difference between a hotel that has data and one that can act on it.*

## AI Economics

**Token**
The unit of text an LLM processes — roughly ¾ of a word. Every AI interaction consumes tokens. More complex reasoning, longer conversations and multiple agents all cost more tokens.
*AI has real costs. Token consumption must be designed for, not discovered after.*

**Token Optimisation**
Designing AI workflows to use the minimum tokens needed to achieve the desired outcome. Smaller purpose-built models for specific tasks instead of always calling large general models.
*Frontier firms are already reigning in agent sprawl. Design for discipline from day one.*

**Agent Sprawl**
What happens when organisations deploy AI agents without governance — costs spiral, complexity grows, and value is unclear. The AI equivalent of unmanaged SaaS subscriptions.
*The sophistication is knowing when NOT to use an agent.*

**Build vs Buy**
The strategic decision of whether to build proprietary AI workflows or purchase out-of-the-box solutions. Buy where it's proven and fast to deploy. Build where your data and workflows create proprietary competitive advantage.
*The data intelligence layer makes both decisions coherent and reversible.*

## The Guest & Commercial Layer

**AI-Native Distribution**
A new model emerging alongside OTAs — hotels appearing directly inside AI conversations with live rates, brand-approved content, and commission-free booking links. Lighthouse Hotels Network (via The Hotels Network) is one of the first live examples.
*In the last era, brands fought for page one on Google. In the next era, they will fight to be the answer inside AI.*

**OTA (Online Travel Agency)**
Booking.com, Expedia, Hotels.com. Third-party platforms that take 15–20% commission per booking. OTAs were ChatGPT launch partners on day one. They are capturing AI bookings right now.
*Hotels without direct AI presence default to OTA bookings — at full commission.*

**Direct Booking**
A reservation made directly with the property — zero commission, full guest data, brand control. AI-native distribution is the mechanism to protect and grow direct bookings in a zero-click world.

**GEO vs SEO — The Key Difference**

| | SEO (Old) | GEO (New) |
|---|---|---|
| Platform | Google | ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity |
| Goal | Rank high in results | Be cited in the answer |
| Format | Links and pages | Structured, machine-readable content |
| Measure | Click-through rate | Citation frequency, share of model |
| Risk | Low ranking | Invisibility — not mentioned at all |

## Governance & Trust

**Responsible AI**
Designing AI systems that are transparent, fair, accountable and respectful of privacy. Not a compliance checkbox — a brand promise to your guests and your team.

**Australian Privacy Act Reforms**
Incoming legislative changes that will affect how guest data is collected, stored, used and disclosed. Being ahead of this is a competitive advantage. Being caught behind it is a brand risk.

**ESG & AI**
Operational data intelligence directly powers sustainability reporting — energy consumption, carbon tracking, waste reduction. If you're working toward loan discount incentives tied to ESG mandates, getting your operational data in order is both a compliance and a financial imperative.

**Consent & Data Governance**
The controls that determine how guest data can be used — what was collected, for what purpose, and who can access it. Must be designed into the data intelligence layer from the start. OneTrust is an example of a platform that manages this.

## Three Things to Do This Week

**1. Test your GEO visibility right now**
Open Claude, ChatGPT and Perplexity. Ask: *"Recommend a luxury boutique hotel for a wellness retreat in [your region]."* See if your property appears — and if it does, whether the description is accurate. This is your GEO baseline.

**2. Audit your technology stack through an API-first lens**
Before signing any new PMS, SaaS platform or app, ask: is it API-first? Does it support MCP connectivity? Vendor lock-in is the enemy of the data flywheel. This review costs nothing and could save millions in future rebuilding costs.

**3. Use AI as your strategic thought partner today**
Open an LLM. Type: *"I am a portfolio manager for a boutique luxury hotel group in Australia transitioning from $400 to $600–800 per night. Help me think through where AI could have the highest impact on guest experience and operational efficiency."* See what emerges. This is the capability you are building.
